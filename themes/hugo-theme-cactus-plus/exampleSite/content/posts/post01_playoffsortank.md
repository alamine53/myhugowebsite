Despite a positive showing in their opening game against the Sixers, the Brooklyn Nets were eliminated just five games into their 2018-19 playoff campaign. One might say a team with no realistic chance of going to the finals would be better off tanking, but the Nets were determined to mark their first playoff appearance since 2013. What benefit, if any, does a short post-season appearance provide? 

<!--more—>

It’s not entirely clear whether weaker teams in the NBA have enough incentive to compete for a playoff ticket. Many of them adopt the tanking route instead. Perhaps a stroke of luck in the draft would provide more bang-on-the-buck than a hopeless playoff push. In reality, even a short playoff appearance does more good than we think.

To demonstrate the returns from post-season experience, let us consider teams around the playoff admission threshold - i.e. 8 seeds and 9 seeds. Although they are close in ability level, their seeding takes them on diverging paths: 9-seeds are sent home with a consolation of a lottery seat in the upcoming draft, and 8-seeds earn a trip to the playoffs for a chance at a championship. 

Historically, 8-seeds have fared miserably in the post-season. Only 5 times in the past 35 years have they gone past the first round. Neither of those materialized in hardware. Some 9-seeds, meanwhile, have had incredible luck in the lottery. [provide example]

One might say that the benefits from being on either side of the threshold balance each other out, but the data suggests otherwise. Looking at following season performance, 8-seeds fare remarkably better. On average, their winning record jumps by 6 percent whereas 9-seeds see a 2 percent reduction.

The question then becomes: where is this improvement coming from? Well, there is no greater test to a team’s potential than a playoff series. Tankers can accumulate as much talent as they like, but if this talent is not put to test then it will vanish. Eventually, the good players are likely to leave for bigger opportunities. One example is the Phoenix Suns who have gathered an impressive collection of talents, yet have struggled to make anything out of it. Soon, Booker and X, their primary talents, will opt out once they see hope for a ring elsewhere. 

Post-season appearance can also help a team’s visibility on the free agent market. Star players are much more likely to choose destinations where they are promised a chance at the playoffs. A few weeks after their first round exit, the Nets were able to sign Durant and Irving, gaining the edge over their next-door rival, the New York Knicks. The Knicks, who have found refuge at the bottom of the standings, have struggled to attract free agents despite being considered the “Mecca of Basketball.”

Next time you think a certain team should be tanking, know that they would miss out on something valuable. Perhaps the momentum will carry them into unprecedented success. Perhaps a star is born in those few games. Perhaps a struggling Point Guard gains confidence from shining on the biggest stage. Perhaps they trade for that missing piece. How ever long it may last, make no mistake that being in the playoffs is better than out of it.
