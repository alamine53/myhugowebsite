A facebook game is circulating where, if nominated, you are asked to post a cover of music albums that were most influential to you. For whatever reason, I feel intrusive to inundate people's feeds with things they neither necessarily are interested in or can benefit from (given that it's only the cover, they can't search for the album if they wanted to). However, I did find the premise of the game enjoyable. Some albums mark important milestones in our lives and we might have a personal attachment to them, so going through the exercise of recalling them is a healthy one. 

For better or for worse, I decided to blog about it - if not for you (the reader) then for me. Why not have a list that I can come back to in the future. Without further ado, here are the top 10 albums that have influenced my taste in music (no particular order).

## 3. 'The Eminem Show' by Eminem (2002)

![eminem](/images/music_albums/TheEminemShow_Eminem.jpg)

**Favorite song:** Superman

I remember listening to this on discman, hoping my mom wouldn't find out what it is. My friends and I also took special pride in that Eminem namedropped Beirut in the song Square Dance, not knowing it was a metaphora for danger (the lyric was accompanied by gun shots and explosive sounds). Still, as with a lot of rebellious kids my age, Eminem was my hero growing up.
